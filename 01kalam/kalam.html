Dr.A.P.J Abdul Kalam

1931 - 2015

Missile Man of India

A.P.J. Abdul Kalam (1931â€“2015) was an Indian aerospace scientist and politician who served as the 11th President of India from 2002 to 2007. Known as the "Missile Man of India" for his work on the country's missile and nuclear weapons programs, he was also fondly called the "People's President".Kalam was born in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, in 1931, into a family with a history of trading that faced financial challenges. To support his family, he sold newspapers as a child while being a dedicated student. He pursued studies in physics and aeronautical engineering, graduating from Madras Institute of Technology in 1957.Kalam served as President of India from 2002 to 2007. Following his presidency, he returned to teaching and writing until his death in 2015. His birthday, October 15, is recognized as World Students' Day by the United Nations.He received India's highest civilian honors for his work, including the Padma Bhushan (1981), Padma Vibhushan (1990), and Bharat Ratna (1997).

Biographies

  • Became the 11th President of India in 2002 and served until 2007.
  • Gained immense popularity during his term, earning the title "People's President".
  • Authored several books, including his autobiography, Wings of Fire.
  • Earned a degree in aeronautical engineering from the Madras Institute of Technology in 1957.
  • Received honorary doctorates from 48 universities.
